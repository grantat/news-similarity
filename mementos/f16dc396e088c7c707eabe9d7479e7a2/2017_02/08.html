    Appellate judges lob critical questions in hearing on travel ban
    A three-judge panel pressed a Justice Department lawyer about the limits on the president’s power, and what evidence was used in temporarily barring refugees and citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries from entering the U.S.
    But the panel similarly interrogated the state of Washington’s solicitor general, who is challenging the travel ban, over whether a lower-court judge’s freeze on the directive was too broad, and what evidence he had to demonstrate religious discrimination in the order.
    A decision on whether to restore the order is expected “probably this week.”
