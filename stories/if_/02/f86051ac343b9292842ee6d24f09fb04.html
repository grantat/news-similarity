Cubs beat Indians 9-3 to force decisive Game 7 in World Series

Photos from Game 6 of the World Series at Progressive Field in Cleveland, Ohio on Nov. 1, 2016.

The Chicago Cubs are alive and kicking.

Their backs against the wall, the Cubs came out swinging and throttled the Cleveland Indians 9-3 in Game 6 of the World Series on Tuesday night in Cleveland. The victory set up a winner-take-all Game 7 on Wednesday night with the Cubs looking to snap a 107-year championship drought.

The heart of the lineup did the damage Tuesday night with Addison Russell knocking in six runs — including a third-inning grand slam that gave the Cubs a 7-0 lead — and Kris Bryant and Anthony Rizzo also homered. Bryant finished with four hits, Rizzo three and Russell and Ben Zobrist each had two.

Jake Arrieta went 5 2/3 innings, yielding two runs on three hits with three walks and nine strikeouts, to earn the victory. Mike Montgomery, Aroldis Chapman, Pedro Strop and Travis Wood came on in relief to seal the deal.

Indians starter Josh Tomlin didn’t make it out of the third inning and allowed six runs on six hits with a walk and no strikeouts.
