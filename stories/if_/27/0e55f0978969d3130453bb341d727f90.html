Conservation is a major theme of this tour, and of Harry's royal patronage. At Lovers Beach, his sunglasses clutched in his teeth, he helped some baby green turtles make their way to the surf. As they flapped their way down the beach, he encouraged with hand gestures exhortations. "Go, go, go! That way!” Paul Edwards, Pool, Getty Images

Boats and birds in the sunny Caribbean; what could be better? Prince Harry tried both on Day 3 of his Caribbean tour, Nov. 22, 2016, traveling through the mangroves on Barbuda to see one of the largest colonies of frigate birds in the world, in Antigua and Barbuda. WPA Pool, Getty Images

None

The night before was more embarrassing for Harry when he was welcomed by Prime Minister Gaston Browne, who teased him about "honeymooning" in the islands with his new girlfriend, American actress Meghan Markle. Cringing ensued. The palace had warned all not to bring up Markle during the tour but Browne wanted to tout tourism to Antigua and Barbuda. WPA Pool, Getty Images

Prince Harry is off to the Caribbean again, departing on a two-week tour on behalf of Queen Elizabeth II that will take him to seven countries: Antigua & Barbuda, St. Kitts and Nevis, St. Lucia, St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Grenada, Barbados and Guyana. But his new girlfriend, American actress Meghan Markle ('Suits'), will not be with him. Instead, Harry spent Sunday, the first day of his visit, with Governor General Sir Rodney Williams at St. John's, Antigua. Samir Hussein, WireImage

None

Prince Harry arrives at V.C. Bird International Airport on the first day of his trip. Chris Jackson, Getty Images

Prince Harry's visit to the Caribbean marks the 35th Anniversary of Independence in Antigua and Barbuda and the 50th Anniversary of Independence in Barbados and Guyana. Chris Jackson, Getty Images

None

The prince was smartly attired on the first day of his official visit in a blue suit, a brightly patterned tie and a light beard. Chris Jackson, Getty Images

None

Prince Harry's last tour of the region was deemed a huge success. This time his tour will include a conservation theme (turtles in Nevis, coral reefs in Grenada), plus meeting local celebrities and sports stars, including Rihanna in her native Barbados. But it's Meghan who's likely to be on everyone's mind. Will Harry spills some beans about his new romance during the tour? Stay tuned. Chris Jackson, Getty Images
