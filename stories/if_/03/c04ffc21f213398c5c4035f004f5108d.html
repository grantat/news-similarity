Cubs' bullpen will be standing room only for Game 7 of World Series

Everyone wants to pitch in Game 7 of the World Series, no matter how tired you are.

Jon Lester and John Lackey will be in the bullpen at some point tonight, hoping for a chance to HELP end the Cubs’ 107-year drought.

“They’re jacked,” David Ross said after Game 6. “They’re as competitive as they get. ‘Lack’ was already talking about it today. ‘Can’t wait. You let me know when I need to go down there,’ and he said he’d run down there.

"Those guys relish the moment to be out on that mound, especially in this environment. Their levels will be really high.”

Unless Kyle Hendricks throws a complete game, who will Joe Maddon call on tonight for the final innings with a championship on the line?

Here are Cubs pitchers and their status for Game 7.

Aroldis Chapman

After throwing 20 pitches in Game 6 following his yeoman 2 2/3 inning outing in Game 5, the question is how much his arm can take without falling off. But Maddon has more trust in Chapman than anyone else in the bullpen, and Chapman wants the ball for that final out. Seems like a certainty at some point if Cubs have a late lead.

Jon Lester

Photos of Cubs starting pitcher Jon Lester.

Possible Cy Young winner and their most consistent starter in the postseason. Helped save the season in his Game 5 start.  Has difficulties throwing to bases, so insertion of Lester may also have to involve insertion of Ross behind the plate to replace Willson Contreras.

John Lackey

Photos of new Cubs pitcher John Lackey.

Hasn’t pitched up to snuff in postseason with 4.85 ERA, but well-rested since Game 4 and was brought to Cubs because of his postseason experience. As all of Chicago knows by now, he didn’t come here for a haircut.

Carl Edwards Jr.

Possible closer of the future has come up big in his rookie season, and has a 1.59 ERA in seven postseason outings. Doesn’t look like a rookie in pressure situations.

Mike Montgomery
