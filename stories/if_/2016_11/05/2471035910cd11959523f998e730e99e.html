Chicago Cubs to make the rounds on TV talk shows next week

Chicago celebrates the Chicago Cubs winning the World Series with a parade from Wrigley Field to Grant Park on Nov. 4, 2016.

Jessi RotiChicago Tribune

Don't be surprised if you see the Cubs making their way onto some TV talk shows next week.

On Monday, first baseman Anthony Rizzo, center fielder Dexter Fowler, and MVP Ben Zobrist will visit "The Tonight Show" with Jimmy Fallon at 10:35 p.m. on NBC.

Third baseman and outfielder Kris Bryant will be on "Jimmy Kimmel Live" at 10:35 p.m. on ABC. Aside from discussing breaking the Cubs' 108-year curse, the pair may swap stories about what growing up in Las Vegas was like.

Bryant will also appear with catcher David Ross on "The Ellen Show" Tuesday at 3 p.m. on NBC.

Hopefully Ellen, who gave two lifelong Cubs fans tickets to Game 4 at Wrigley Field, has another Chicago-themed game for Bryant and Ross to compete in on Tuesday's show.
