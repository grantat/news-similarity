USA TODAY Sports 8:04 p.m. EST November 23, 2016

USA TODAY Sports breaks down how the NFL playoffs would look if the regular season ended today. USA TODAY Sports

Josh Norman and Dez Bryant(Photo: Evan Russell, USA TODAY Sports)

Thanksgiving is here and with it a three-game NFL appetizer to start Week 12.

First place is on the line in the NFC North as the Detroit Lions, per tradition, open this week's slate by hosting the Minnesota Vikings, who lost to this team in Week 9. Next up, the Dallas Cowboys look to run their league-best record to 10-1, while extending their franchise-record nine-game winning streak by putting further distance between themselves and the surging Washington Redskins. Finally, Thursday night offers a matchup between two .500 teams the Pittsburgh Steelers and Indianapolis Colts in what could be a de facto elimination game, at least in terms of the AFC wild-card race.

Sunday's action is capped by the Kansas Chiefs' visit to the Denver Broncos, a game flexed into the Sunday night slot and another contest with important playoff ramifications. The Green Bay Packers head to Philadelphia to end the week as they and the Eagles struggle to remain relevant in the NFC.
