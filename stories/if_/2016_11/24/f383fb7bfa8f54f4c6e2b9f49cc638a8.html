    Cruise ship Adonia, from the new Carnival's Fathom line, arrives in Havana on May 2, 2016.(Photo: Alejandro Ernesto, European Pressphoto Agency)

    The startup cruise line that grabbed headlines this year by launching voyages from Miami to Cuba will cease sailings by the summer of 2017.

    Miami-based Fathom will return its single ship, the 704-passenger Adonia, to British line P&O Cruises, which previously operated the vessel, according to a statement from Fathom's parent company, Carnival Corp.

    Fathom just began operations in April.

    Carnival Corp. launched Fathom with a bold mission to operate social impact-focused cruises, initially to the Dominican Republic. The company later widened its mission to include the Cuba sailings, which began in May.

    USA TODAY

    Cuba cruises on hold at Royal Caribbean

    While the Cuba sailings were popular, the social impact-focused trips to the Dominican Republic never caught on. The trips revolved around volunteer activities such as teaching English in schools, helping to cultivate cacao plants and building water filtration systems.

    In announcing plans for Fathom in 2015, Carnival Corp. said the line would cater to an under-served market of consumers who want to have a positive impact on people's lives. The company cited research that said it was a sizable and growing market.

    Carnival Corp. spokesman Roger Frizzell told USA TODAY that Fathom will continue as an entity that operates social impact-focused shore tours in the Dominican Republic. The tours will be available to passengers on ships operated by other Carnival Corp.-owned brands. The company also may expand the social impact-focused shore tour operation to other destinations beyond the Dominican Republic.

    Carnival Corp. hopes to continue Cuba sailings beyond next summer with other brands. Frizzell said the company already has requested approval from the Cuban government for other brands to sail there beginning in June 2017.

    Fathom's Cuba sailings were "extremely successful," Frizzell said.
