OSHA: Snowplow killed worker at Deep Tunnel site in Summit

Fire truck image
Tribune illustration
Tribune illustration
Liam Ford and Erin GallagherContact ReporterChicago Tribune

A man who died Monday in Summit at the site of a Deep Tunnel stormwater reservoir had been struck by vehicle that was part of a snowplowing operation, the federal Occupational Safety and Health Administration said Tuesday morning.

The man, a carpenter craft worker, died about 2 p.m. at the McCook Reservoir project, part of the Deep Tunnel system, said Bob Kula, a spokesman for the man's employer, Kiewit Corp. of Omaha. Kiewit is the primary contractor on the $121 million main tunnel and gate portion of the $638 million project.

A news release Tuesday from OSHA said it was investigating the death of the man, who was identified as Robert Ellison, 55, of the 39300 block of North Hickory Street in Spring Grove, according to the Cook County medical examiner's office. The cause and manner of his death had not yet been determined by the medical examiner.

In its news release, OSHA said Ellison was "struck and killed by a rough-terrain vehicle during snowplowing operations" in a parking lot of the Metropolitan Water Reclamation District site at 7700 Canal Bank Road in the southwest suburb. OSHA spokesman Scott Allen said the vehicle had a plow on it.

A member of Ellison's family did not wish to comment when contacted Tuesday.

OHSA is part of the U.S. Department of Labor. Allen said the agency's investigation will determine who was operating the vehicle and who employed that person. Allen did not immediately know if Ellison was working at the time of the incident, or if he was struck in the parking lot before or after work.

"That's part of the investigation we're still trying to determine," Allen said. "We're conducting interviews with the employer, other employees and potential witnesses."

OSHA in its release said that "the danger of being struck by vehicles increases in low-visibility and slippery condition."

Kiewit released a company statement Monday saying: "We were deeply saddened to learn that an employee on the McCook Reservoir Main Tunnel System project died earlier today. We extend our sincerest condolences to his family and friends, and those who knew and worked with him at Kiewit. Our deepest thoughts and prayers are with all of them at this time."

The company is working with the Army Corps of Engineers and others involved in the project, as well as authorities, to investigate the death, according to the statement.

According to OSHA, Kiewit is a Fortune 300 company that operates through a network of offices and projects in the United States, Canada and Australia.

A spokeswoman for the Metropolitan Water Reclamation District said in a statement: "We certainly recognize this as a tragic loss, and our thoughts and prayers go out to the family."
