Week 14 live blog: Bears' final drive falls short, Lions win 20-17

Photos from the Bears-Lions game in Detroit on Dec. 11, 2016, at Ford Field.

Two holding penalties on the Bears’ final drive left them short Sunday at Ford Field, as the Detroit Lions held on to a 20-17 win and preserved their first-place cushion in the NFC North.

 

Matthew Stafford’s 7-yard scramble for a touchdown was the decisive blow with 3 minutes, 17 seconds to go. Against a four-man rush, Stafford scanned the field, then took off when pressured. He side-stepped linebacker John Timu at about the 4-yard line.

 

The Bears (3-10) forced the Lions to come from behind by intercepting Stafford twice in the fourth quarter. In the process, they ended their streak of 18 quarters without an interception or fumble recovery.

 

On the second interception, rookie Cre’Von LeBlanc was physical against veteran Anquan Boldin, then undercut an out route. Le’Blanc raced 24 yards to the end zone, holding the ball above his head and high-stepping the last 10 yards. It gave the Bears a 17-13 lead with 7:07 to play.

 

The Bears tried to drive from their 25 into field goal range. Holding penalties against Charles Leno, then Ted Larsen, negated back-to-back completion of 27 and 23 yards into Lions territory. That set up a climactic fourth-and-11 from the Lions’ 44. Barkley through deep down the middle. The ball got into Josh Bellamy’s body, but with Darius Slay on his hip, Bellamy couldn’t catch it. 

—Rich Campbell
