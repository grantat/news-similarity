    Delta jet slides into grass at snowy Detroit airport

    Today in the Sky

    Ben Mutzabaugh , USA TODAY 8:37 p.m. EST December 11, 2016
    2.6k Shares

    This image, provided to USA TODAY by Delta passenger Isaac Price via Twitter, shows passengers using the emergency exit to leave a plane that became stuck in the grass at Detroit's airport on Dec. 11, 2016. (Photo: Courtesy of Isaac Price (@FFMBA3N) via Twitter)

    A Delta Air Lines flight slid into the grass as it was taxiing after landing at the Detroit Metro Airport on Sunday morning.

    Delta confirmed the incident to Today in the Sky, saying it occurred around 11:30 a.m. ET on a flight arriving from Buffalo.

    "Delta Flight 724 ... landed and was turning from the runway onto the taxiway when it left the pavement, stopping in the snowy grass," Delta spokesman Anthony Black said in a statement. "The passengers were off-loaded and bused to the terminal about an hour late. All bags were recovered."

    Delta said none of the 65 passengers or five crew members were injured.

    The incidenthappened as snow fell in Detroit and at other airports across the Great Lakes region. It was not immediately clear how much the snow might have contributed to the mishap, though the storm had forced airlines to ground more than 1,500 flights in the region. Following the incident, a "ground stop" was put in place by the Federal Aviation Administration for flights at other airports awaiting take off for Detroit. The agency cited "WEATHER/SNOW-ICE and WEATHER/BRAKING ACTION" as the ground stop remained in effect as of 3:20 p.m. ET.

    Delta spokesman Black said the airline has launched an investigation to determine exactly what caused the plane to leave the pavement.

    Numerous passengers from Delta Flight 724 took to social media with tales of the incident; many included photos from the scene.

    We got to exit off the tail of the plane. That was kind of cool, a passenger identified on Twitter as Isaac Price said in a tweet sent at 12:12 p.m. ET.
