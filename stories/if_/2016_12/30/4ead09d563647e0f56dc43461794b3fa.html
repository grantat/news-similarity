College campus provocateur Milo Yiannopoulos just got a huge book deal

By Holly Epstein Ojalvo 2:29 pm EST December 29, 2016

421 shares
SHARE
TWEET
SMS
SEND
EMAIL

Milo Yiannopoulos is a conservative columnist and Internet personality. (Photo: Drew Angerer, Getty Images)

Milo Yiannopoulos courts controversy pretty much wherever he goes.

He’s been called a provocateur, a troll, the poster boy of the alt-right and a white nationalist. He’s even called himself the “supervillain” of the Internet.

And now he has a big fat book deal — along with his existing plan to make a movie with footage from his controversial tour of college campuses.

Wait — who is Milo Yiannopoulos?

If you’re not familiar with Yiannopoulos, here are some factoids about him:

  • He’s a British writer and public speaker.
  • He’s 32 years old, Catholic and openly gay.
  • He founded the web magazine the Kernel, which Daily Dot Media bought in 2014.
  • He’s an editor for the conservative publication Breitbart News.
  • He’s a fan of Donald Trump (who he has called “Daddy”) and criticizes identity politics and feminism.
  • He often makes provocative statements some people find offensive and defends himself vigorously on free speech grounds.
  • He got kicked off Twitter for his role in the trolling of actress Leslie Jones.

What’s this about him having a book deal?

Simon & Schuster is a book publisher, and they just gave Yiannopoulos a book deal for $250,000, according to the Hollywood Reporter.

Milo Yiannopoulos strikes $250K book deal https://t.co/ryOPCPWymI pic.twitter.com/IWfMJYJCr7

— Hollywood Reporter (@THR) December 29, 2016

Yiannopoulos told the Hollywood Reporter,

“I met with top execs at Simon & Schuster earlier in the year and spent half an hour trying to shock them with lewd jokes and outrageous opinions. I thought they were going to have me escorted from the building — but instead they offered me a wheelbarrow full of money.”

And he posted about his deal on Facebook, saying, “I’m more powerful, more influential, and more fabulous than ever before and this book is the moment Milo goes mainstream. Social justice warriors should be scared — very scared.”

And his movie?

Yiannopoulos has been hitting college campuses around the country, and is often met with vocal students protesting his message.

Related: Conservative journo Milo Yiannopoulos loves to ‘shock and outrage’ college students

His stock response to these protests is to say that liberals are trying to quash his freedom of speech. And he often mocks his critics too.

The Hollywood Reporter reported last summer that Yiannopoulos was working on a documentary based on his campus tour, but nothing new has come out about that lately.

Another documentary, American Milo, was already made about him.

What are people saying about all this?

There’s quite a lot of outrage on the left. And some clapback from the right.
