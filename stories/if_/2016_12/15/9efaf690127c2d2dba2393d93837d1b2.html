    Traveling Tips From Monica Drake, The Times’s Travel Editor

    On Wednesday, Dec. 14, Monica Drake, The New York Times’s travel editor, was on hand for a live chat about travel. DEC. 7, 2016

    1. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:27 PM ET

      Hi, everyone! I’m Monica, The New York Times’s travel editor.

    2. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:27 PM ET

      I’m on hand for the next hour to discuss holiday travel do’s and dont’s, to share travel tips, and to offer recommendations about the world’s great, hot, hidden and off-the-radar destinations.

    3. AF

      Alex F

      Reader, Phoenix, AZ

      2:37 PM ET

      Hi Monica! My fiancee and I are planning our honeymoon in Cancun. We wanted somewhere warm, not too far away and safe. Do you have any pitfalls we should avoid while planning and traveling there? Any suggestions for activities?

    4. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:36 PM ET

      First of all, congratulations on your upcoming wedding!

    5. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:36 PM ET

      I would urge you to try to go off the beaten path a bit. We published a great 36 Hours on Cancun a few years back in which we suggested a day trip to Isla Mujeres:

      36 Hours in Cancún, Mexico

      There’s more to Mexico’s top travel destination than alcohol-fueled partying. Take a look.

      The New York Times

    7. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:37 PM ET

      The other option is to go farther afield and check out Tulum, which has grown more touristy but has still retained the charm that made it popular.

    8. CH

      Caroline Hamburger

    9. Monica Drake

    10. Monica Drake

    11. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      36 Hours on the Beach in Barcelona

      The New York Times

    14. J

      Judy

      Reader, San Francisco, CA

      Hi! I love long train trips. Do you have any recommendations for great vacations by rail?

    15. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:46 PM ET

      Thanks for this question. Trains are a great way to explore an expansive country. I’m assuming you’re going domestic.

    17. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:47 PM ET

      Nina Burleigh wrote a great piece on the California Zephyr in which she traces a route she took as a kid.

    18. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:50 PM ET

      Taking Amtrak’s California Zephyr Across Country

      See what a writer saw as she recreated an eye-opening rail adventure that took her family to the Summer of Love.

      The New York Times

    19. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:48 PM ET

      Another writer took a train trip from New Orleans to Los Angeles to retrace the Great Migration of African-Americans from the South.

    20. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:48 PM ET

      My advice would be to find a route that you want to retrace since that will give your trip more meaning. And if the landscape is the American West, you’re going to see some spellbinding scenery from your window.

    21. M

      Thanks!

    22. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:51 PM ET

      Hi Maryann — You’ve chosen a very popular time to visit. I’m not sure which city you’re going to but in Cape Town there are plenty of under the radar places to go. There’s a market at an old biscuit mill, for example, that probably has more character than more luxury options.

    23. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:54 PM ET

      36 Hours in Cape Town

      A plan for this stunning city might include a 5K run (or walk), browsing farm markets, dinner at a wine estate and, no surprise, heading up Table Mountain (by cable car).

      The New York Times

    24. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:53 PM ET

      But there are also a lot of hybrid shops that sell tchotchkes and art and food as well.

    25. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:55 PM ET

      And I’d be remiss not to point out the wonders of traveling Africa by road. Jeffrey Gettleman, a longtime correspondent, just drove from Kenya to Cape Town. His journey is pretty inspiring.

    26. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:55 PM ET

      Seeing Africa by Road

      A 4,250-mile, 16-day trip through six countries with five children and four hardheaded adults. What could go wrong?

      The New York Times

    33. D

      David

      Reader, San Francisco, CA

      2:58 PM ET

      In your opinion, do folks tend to overplan or underplan trips (activities, itineraries, etc.)? Given the choice between the two, which would you recommend?

    34. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:59 PM ET

      I’m going to blame this on being in New York — everyone I know tends to overplan their trips.

    35. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      2:59 PM ET

      I’m including myself in that camp.

    36. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:00 PM ET

      I’d recommend not only underplanning but also occasionally spinning the globe, so to speak, try a place you’re not familiar with and do all your planning offline, without using reviews as a filter.

    37. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:00 PM ET

      A great tool to use to do this is Google flights and also Kayak, which allows you to put in a budget and figure out where you go based on price and proximity: https://www.kayak.com/explore/

      Penny

      Where are some adventurous places to travel right now that are relatively safe, particularly with so much uncertainty and political upheaval happening in the world.

    48. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:10 PM ET

      Hi Penny. I’m assuming that you’re looking for camping/hiking and not unexplored terrain. Before I give you a couple of destinations, I will say that we are more connected than ever before, so we’re a bit more aware of upheaval than we used to be. We’ve run a couple of essays on risk that will inspire a bit of courage.

    49. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:10 PM ET

      An Informed Traveler Is a Safer Traveler

      Zika and terrorism targeting tourist areas have travelers on edge. Tips on how to stay safe, and sane, on the road.

      The New York Times

    50. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:10 PM ET

      An Argument for Travel During Turbulent Times

      The recent disaster-in-installments in Japan of earthquake, tsunami, damaged nuclear reactors and near-meltdown is a particular shock; Japan has long been regarded as one of the safest countries in the world. And now it seems a perilous place of inundated cities and contaminated air and undrinkable water.

      The New York Times

    51. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:11 PM ET

      So keep in mind that to be a traveler means embracing adventure and that one of the riskiest things you can do is to get behind the wheel of a car during a commute.

    52. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:13 PM ET

      As for a destination, I’d like to try the Dry Tortugas, which is remote and requires camping. We did a piece on it in August:

      West of Key West, an Under-the-Radar National Park

      Sea, sand, history and climate forecasts are all part of a trip to the Dry Tortugas, an aquatic paradise at the edge of the Gulf of Mexico.

      The New York Times

    53. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      Michael

      Reader, Portland, OR

    71. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:29 PM ET

      You may want to try to two practices that I do when I’m traveling even with people I very much get along with: 1) Bookmark time away and be up front about it. Not rude time away, not like everyone’s at a huge dinner and you get up and go to the other room, but time away, as in: “Every morning I like to get up at the crack of dawn and watch the sun rise while I contemplate life — alone.” Or, if they’re early risers, maybe it’s the moon rise in the evening. Find out how to do it but it’s essential.

    72. Monica Drake

      Travel Editor, The New York Times

      3:29 PM ET

      2) Exercise. I truly believe that it helps jetlag and other factors that make you more irritable while traveling.
