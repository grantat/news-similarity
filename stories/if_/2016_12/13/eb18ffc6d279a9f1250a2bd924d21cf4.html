    60. From a Reader

      I am a purist and have always maintained that latkes cannot be made in advance, and have to be eaten directly from the fry pan. But then one is standing and cooking during the party. if you have to make in advance, what is the best way to keep warm in the oven? Brown paper? A rack?

      Anna

      New York, NY

    61. Kim Severson

      Food Reporter, The New York Times

      2:44 PM ET

      Well, I’m a Catholic in love with a Jew and her huge Jewish family. I do this version, which they all seem to love. You make your latkes as you do — I like a recipe that has both potatoes shredded in the food processor and some chopped in the food processor with the onion — then par-fry them. You want them just turning golden and holding together. Drain on paper towels then freeze on cookie sheets. Put the latkes in a freezer bag. When ready to serve, preheat the oven to 425. Arrange the frozen latkes on baking sheets, in a single layer without crowding. Bake for about 15-20 minutes, turning once, until deep golden brown. Drain briefly on a paper towel-lined platter. Serve hot.
