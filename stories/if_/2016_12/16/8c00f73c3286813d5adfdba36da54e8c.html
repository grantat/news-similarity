Boston U. student raises $700 for homeless man just before Christmas

By Brianne Garrett, Boston University 4:21 pm EST December 15, 2016

112 shares
SHARE
TWEET
SMS
SEND
EMAIL

Blake Smith, a Boston University junior, turned a class assignment into a Christmas gift.

His task? “Create a video that persuades people to think about something differently” for a communications course. Smith’s immediate idea was to craft a video showcasing a man he knows as Freddy, a friendly homeless man in Boston’s Kenmore Square.

Blake Smith surprised Freddy with money that he raised from GoFundMe. (Photo: Courtesy of Blake Smith)

Freddy is known for “always sending good vibes” to people who walk by him, said Smith, who passes Freddy every day on the way to class. The two developed a quick friendship.

“A lot of people just kind of ignore homeless people or think they’re lazy and that’s why they’re in that position,” Smith told USA TODAY College. “But Freddy sits out in the cold at 30 degrees – that’s not someone who’s lazy. That’s someone who walks all the way from the Boston hospital, because there’s a shelter there that he’s at, and he walks all the way to Kenmore just to get a couple of dollars every day for food.”

Smith’s video tells the story of a young wealthy character who walks past Freddy — who plays himself in the short film — every day, throwing out leftovers and then going home to a fridge full of food. Later in the video, the wealthy character has a change of heart and offers his takeout meal to Freddy. The video ends with the two men sitting down to share the food together.

“I learned from (making) this video to be grateful for what you have,” said Smith. “Maybe you don’t need the new iPhone, but you could help somebody else. Take the time to stop and talk to somebody, see how their position is in life.”

But Smith’s efforts did not stop at creating the video. He also started a GoFundMe page, with the hope of raising money for Freddy, as a holiday gift. In nine days, Smith raised $700 for Freddy — just $300 short of his final goal.

“He gave me a huge hug and was really happy for everything,” said Smith. “He was overjoyed, and he told me that he was going to go get a room and get out (of) the cold and try to get a place to stay for a little while.”

Freddy’s reaction was exactly what Smith was hoping for.

“That’s what it was for, basically,” Smith said. “To help him out for a while because no one should have to sit outside during the Christmas season.”

Related: More than $70k raised for homeless student who biked six hours to college

It may have started off as an idea for a project, but Smith said the gesture was about much more than a grade. He hopes to continue impacting lives like Freddy’s with his social activism documentary class next semester.

“I didn’t want to just make a video to get a good grade — I was kind of sick of doing projects that way,” said Smith. “I wanted to do something that could help someone outside of class.”

Brianne Garrett is a Boston University student and a USA TODAY College correspondent.
