    Sports in 2016: Winners, Losers and Cheaters

    By THE NEW YORK TIMES DEC. 21, 2016

    From a baseball player turned hit man to the amazing ability of Simone Biles, a look at some of the most compelling stories of the year.

    1. By SARAH LYALL
      Swim. Bike. Cheat?

      April 10 — Cheating in sports took a strange turn when Julie Miller, an age-group champion at the 2015 Ironman Canada, was disqualified after detective work by athletes who believed she had cut the course there and at other races. Read more

      “We can’t prove what happened on the course in Ironman Canada in 2015, or what her intent was. People can make their own judgments and decisions.” - Keats McGonigal, regional director for Ironman
    2. By ALAN SCHWARZ, WALT BOGDANICH and JACQUELINE WILLIAMS
      N.F.L.’s Flawed Concussion Research and Ties to Tobacco Industry

      March 24 - An investigation by The New York Times raised questions about the N.F.L.’s concussion research and its relationship with the tobacco industry. Read more

      “You’re not doing science here; you are putting forth some idea that you already have.” - Bill Barr, neuropsychologist
    3. By ROBERT LIPSYTE
      The Death of Muhammad Ali

      June 4 — Ali was the most thrilling if not the best heavyweight boxer of all time, a towering figure with an agile mind who was both admired and vilified for his religious, political and social stances. Read more

      “I don’t have to be who you want me to be; I’m free to be who I want.” - Ali
    4. By CHRISTOPH FUHRMANS, ANH-THU HUYNH, JIM LUTTRELL, SAM MANCHESTER, RUMSEY TAYLOR, and JOSH WILLIAMS
      ‘A Front-Row Seat. To Misery.’

      April 1 — Shock. Anger. Agony. Pain. Behold the faces of Mets fans in this picture, taken by Chang W. Lee of The New York Times as the Royals’ Eric Hosmer dived safely home to tie the score in the ninth inning of Game 5 of last year’s World Series. Three innings later, the Royals scored five runs to win the championship. We managed to track down 11 of the people in the photo, and we asked those with the best view of the play to relive it. Read more

      “I think it’s something that I couldn’t even replicate. It’s almost not even a human emotion. Just absolute despair.”- Dan DeBernardi, Mets fan
    5. By KAREN CROUSE
      Seeking Answers, Michael Phelps Finds Himself

      June 26 — Michael Phelps’s road to becoming the most decorated athlete in Olympic history had been treacherously steep and single-file narrow, as isolating as a deep free diver’s plunge. But before he finished his record-setting career at the Rio Games, he had to overcome his own issues by attending rehab. Read more

      “He hid everything that makes him human for 12 years. The rehab is what opened him up.” - Bob Bowman, Phelps’s coach
    6. By REBECCA R. RUIZ and MICHAEL SCHWIRTZ
      How Russia’s State-Run Doping Fueled Olympic Gold

      May 13 — Dozens of Russian athletes at the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi, including at least 15 medal winners, were part of a state-run doping program, meticulously planned for years to ensure dominance at the Games, according to Grigory Rodchenkov, the director of the country’s antidoping laboratory at the time. Read more

      “People are celebrating Olympic champion winners, but we are sitting crazy and replacing their urine.” - Dr. Rodchenkov
    7. By DAN BARRY
      A Fight, Followed by Tragedy

      March 28 — Two men entered the ring for their first professional fight, a four-rounder in the hall of a Ukrainian church in Youngstown. What happened next would change everyone. Read more

      “You knew. You knew right then and there.” - Jack Loew, boxing coach
    8. By THE NEW YORK TIMES
      The World’s Best Gymnast

      Aug. 5 — The question wasn’t if she was going to win a gold medal, but how many. Heading into the Rio Games we took a look at what made Simone Biles so good. Read more

      “Normally the separations between first and second place could be three-tenths or five-tenths, and she goes out and wins by one or two points.” - Nastia Lukin, 2008 gold medalist
    10. By DAVE ANDERSON
      The Death of Arnold Palmer

      Sept. 25 — Palmer was the champion golfer whose full-bore style of play, thrilling tournament victories and magnetic personality inspired an American golf boom, attracted a following known as Arnie’s Army and made him one of the most popular athletes in the world. Read more

      “Arnold popularized the game. He gave it a shot in the arm when the game needed it.” - Jack Nicklaus
    11. By DAN BARRY
      The Big League Prospect Who Became a Mob Hit Man

      Oct. 30 — Baseball scouts used to scrutinize Maury Lerner’s every move. Then F.B.I. agents were the ones watching. Lerner batted .328 for the Boise Braves in 1957. Twenty years later, he was serving time at the Adult Correctional Institutions in Rhode Island. Read more

      “One of those should-have-beens. An American tragedy, when you think of it.” - Glen, Lerner’s son
    12. By LESLYE DAVIS, ALEXANDRA GARCIA and SERGIO PEÇANHA
      The Power of Gold

      Aug. 21 — In 2012, the Brazilian judoka Rafaela Silva was a medal favorite, but she was disqualified. At the Rio Games, she sought to show that her humble background could not squelch her dreams. Watch

      “Things changed, like water to wine. That medal has a lot of power. It does.” - Luis Carlos, Silva’s father
    13. By JERÉ LONGMAN
      Could Someone Run a Marathon Under Two Hours?

      May 16 — The attempt to identify promising talent for an audacious plan: To use science to facilitate a sub-two-hour marathon by 2019, a decade or two sooner than many experts think possible. Read more

      “You’re here once. You want to do something that matters.” - Yannis Pitsiladis, physiologist
