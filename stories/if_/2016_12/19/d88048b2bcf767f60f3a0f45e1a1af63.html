Brock Osweiler benched by Texans, replaced by Tom Savage

Lorenzo Reyes , USA TODAY 2:24 p.m. EST December 18, 2016

Houston Texans quarterback Brock Osweiler (17) attempts a pass as Jacksonville Jaguars outside linebacker Telvin Smith (50) applies pressure during the first quarter at NRG Stadium.(Photo: Troy Taormina, USA TODAY Sports)

48 CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMORE

High-priced free agent quarterback Brock Osweiler has been benched by the Houston Texans.

In light of Osweiler's continued struggles with accuracy issues, Texans coach Bill O’Brien pulled his regular starter in favor for third-year veteran Tom Savage in the second quarter of Sunday's contest against the Jacksonville Jaguars.

Prior to his benching, Osweiler completed just six of 11 passes on the day for 48 yards, two interceptions and no touchdowns.

The Texans signed Osweiler to a four-year, $72 million contract this offseason with $37 million guaranteed in the first two seasons, meaning that Houston could theoretically cut its losses following Osweiler's second-season with the team.

Entering Sunday’s game against the 2-11 Jaguars, Osweiler had completed just 59.7% of his passes for 2,656 yards, 14 touchdowns and 14 interceptions.

Savage’s first attempt was a 32-yard connection with rookie Wendall Williams. He eventually led Houston on a scoring drive to cut the Jaguars lead. But the drive ended when Savage's fourth-and-goal fade attempt to DeAndre Hopkins fell incomplete.
