Jaguars fire Gus Bradley after sinking to 2-12

Lorenzo Reyes , USA TODAY 11:55 p.m. EST December 18, 2016
Skip in Skip

The MMQB's Peter King reacts to the firing of Jacksonville Jaguars head coach Gus Bradley. Time_Sports

Jacksonville Jaguars head coach Gus Bradley yells out during the second half of an NFL football game against the Minnesota Vikings at EverBank Field.(Photo: Reinhold Matay, USA TODAY Sports)

There are now two head coaching vacancies in the NFL.

After blowing a 13-point lead in a loss Sunday, the Jacksonville Jaguars announced they have fired Gus Bradley.

The Jaguars fell to 2-12 after losing 21-20 to the Houston Texans. With the loss, Bradleys career record in Jacksonville fell to 14-48 over nearly four full seasons. That makes him the head coach with the lowest winning percentage in the Super Bowl era (.226) with a minimum of 50 games coached.

The Jaguars were a popular preseason pick to compete for an AFC South title, but quarterback Blake Bortles regressed and consistently compromised a talented defense.

USA TODAY

Team owner Shad Khan announced the move in a statement shortly after the loss.

I thanked Gus Bradley today for his commitment to the Jacksonville Jaguars over the past four seasons, Khan's statement read. As anyone close to our team knows, Gus gave his staff and players literally everything he had. Our players competed for Gus and I know they have great respect for him, as do I.

Gus also represented the Jaguars, the Jacksonville community and the NFL in nothing less than a first-class manner as our head coach. That counts for a lot. It is unfortunately evident that we must make a change. I thought it would be best to do it immediately after todays result so Gus can step away, relax and regroup with his family during the Christmas and holiday season.

(General manager) Dave Caldwell agreed and will now be charged with exploring all options to hire the best head coach possible to lead what I feel is an extremely talented team and reward a very loyal and patient fan base in Jacksonville.

That Caldwell is mentioned in Khan's statement indicates the Jaguars will probably not be undergoing a complete overhaul of the front office.

One potential candidate to take over for Bradley on an interim basis is assistant head coach Doug Marrone, who also oversee the offensive line. He compiled a 15-17 record in two seasons as head coach of the Buffalo Bills in 2013-14.

Bradley joins Jeff Fisher, whom the Los Angeles Rams fired Dec. 12, as former head coaches who will be looking for new roles this offseason.
