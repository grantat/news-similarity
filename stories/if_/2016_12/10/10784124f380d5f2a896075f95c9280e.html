T.J. Miller arrested two days before hosting Critics Choice Awards

The Associated Press 7:47 p.m. EST December 9, 2016

T.J. Miller at premiere of 'Office Christmas Party' in Los Angeles, Dec. 7, 2016.(Photo: Jordan Strauss/Invision/AP)

LOS ANGELES Actor/comedian T.J. Miller, star of Silicon Valley and the just-opened Office Christmas Party, was arrested early Friday after a car-service driver accused him of battery, Los Angeles police say.

Miller's arrest came two days before he will host the Critics' Choice Awards in Santa Monica on Sunday.

Officer Drake Madison says Miller was arrested around 1a.m. Friday. The accusing driver wanted Miller booked under a citizen's arrest.

Madison did not have additional details about what led to the dispute between the driver and Miller.

TMZ reported that Miller and the driver got into an argument about President-elect Donald Trump, and Miller allegedlyslapped the driver in the head when they arrived at Miller's home.

Kate Gorney and T.J. Miller at Vulture Awards Season Party on Dec. 8, 2016 in West Hollywood. (Photo: Tommaso Boddi, Getty Images for New York Magazi)

Miller was no fan of Trump, calling him a "madman" and "lunatic" on Twitter in July.

Madison says Miller was released on his own recognizance and was ordered to appear in court on Jan. 9.

Joey Berlin, president of the organization that bestows the Critics' Choice Awards, says Miller will remain the host for Sunday's show.

"We look forward to seeing what he's going to do on Sunday," Berlin said.

A call to Miller's manager was not immediately returned.

Miller is best known for his role as Erlich Bachman in the quirky Silicon Valley, inwhich he plays the owner of a home that serves as headquarters for a fledgling start-up.

He's also in the comedy Office Christmas Party,which opened Friday. Heco-stars withJason Bateman,Olivia MunnandKate McKinnonas fun-loving employees whothrowa debaucherous, R-rated holiday party to save their tech company.
