4 killed, 7 wounded in shootings in Chicago

Four men were killed and seven other people were wounded in shootings in Chicago from early Thursday through early Friday, according to police.

Joseph Anderson, 42, died after he was shot in the face inside a former grocery store on the Far South Side just after 10:30 p.m. He was pronounced dead at the scene in the 400 block of East 107th Street in the Rosemoor neighborhood, police said.

Neighbors said the building has been vacant for about two years. There is an apartment upstairs, and the building is just feet away from Cullen Elementary School.

Anderson lived on the block where he was shot, according to the Cook County medical examiner.

Earlier Thursday also on the Far South Side, a 22-year-old man was killed and a 19-year-old man was seriously wounded around 1:15 p.m. in the 11300 block of South Edbrooke Avenue in the Roseland neighborhood, police said.

The 22-year-old was shot in the back and was pronounced dead at Advocate Christ Medical Center in Oak Lawn, according to Officer Kevin Quaid, a police spokesman. The 19-year-old was taken in serious condition to Roseland Community Hospital with a wound to the elbow, Quaid said.

About 11:45 a.m. on the South Side, a man in his 20s was shot to death in the Gresham neighborhood, police said.  The man was hit in the abdomen in the 8300 block of South Hamilton Avenue, said Officer Nicole Trainor, a police spokeswoman.

Early Thursday morning, Elijah Murphy, 38, was shot in the head and chest as he was being dropped off for work at a warehouse in the 4200 block of South Racine Avenue around 4:45 a.m., according to police.

Murphy was pronounced dead at 5:15 a.m. on the scene, according to the medical examiner. Police said a dark-colored SUV was seen speeding from the area. No one was in custody.

Other shootings:

• About 4 a.m. Friday on the Northwest Side, a 32-year-old man was shot in the leg after two men approached him in the 3200 block of North Austin Avenue in the Belmont Central neighborhood and demanded his phone and money, police said.  The man said he didn't have money or a phone, and one of the robbers shot him. He was taken to Community First Medical Center, then transferred to Illinois Masonic Medical Center, where his condition was stabilized.

• About 11:50 p.m. Thursday on the West Side, a 24-year-old man was hit in the left shoulder in a drive-by shooting in the Humboldt Park neighborhood. He was walking in the 2600 block of West Division Street when someone inside a black sedan fired at him. He went took himself to Norwegian Hospital, and he was later transferred to Stroger Hospital, where his condition was stabilized.

• About 10:55 p.m. on the South Side, a 41-year-old man was shot in the groin while he was standing in the 800 block of East 88th Street in the Burnside neighborhood, police said. Two people approached the man and fired at him. He was taken to Northwestern Memorial Hospital, where his condition was stabilized.

• Just before 10:30 p.m. on the South Side, a 24-year-old man was shot in the left arm in an alley in the 1200 block of West 60th Street in the Englewood neighborhood, police said. He went to St. Bernard Hospital, where his condition was stabilized.

• About 7:55 p.m. on the West Side, a man was shot during a robbery in the 3000 block of West Fillmore Street in the Lawndale neighborhood, police said. He was sitting in a vehicle when someone approached and announced a robbery, shooting the man in the arm and the leg. The victim drove to the 11th District police station. He was taken in good condition to Mount Sinai Hospital.

• About 11:30 a.m., a person was shot in the Lawndale neighborhood, Quaid said. That shooting happened in the 3900 block of West Grenshaw Street. The victim was taken to Mount Sinai Hospital where he was listed in good condition, Quaid said.
