Could Chip Kelly be the Jacksonville Jaguars new offensive coordinator?

The team has yet to announce who will call the plays under new coach Doug Marrone, and Kelly the former Philadelphia Eagles and San Francisco 49ers coach was at Jaguars headquarters on Monday, a person with knowledge of the situation confirmed to USA TODAY Sports. The person spoke on condition of anonymity because the meeting had not been announced.

Kellys visit to Jaguars headquarters was first reported by 1010XL in Jacksonville. ESPN reported over the weekend that Kelly quietly interviewed for the Jaguars head coaching job before the team decided to promote Marrone, who finished the season as interim coach.

USA TODAY

Josh McDaniels removes name from consideration for 49ers' coaching job

Installing Kellys up-tempo scheme would be a bold move by the Jaguars, who have some talent on that side of the ball, starting with receivers Allen Robinson and Allen Hurns, and hope quarterback Blake Bortles can rebound from an uneven third season.

The 49ers fired Kelly, 53, on Jan. 1, hours after their season finale. They went 2-14 in his only season there after three seasons with the Philadelphia Eagles, who fired Kelly with a 6-9 record late in 2015.
