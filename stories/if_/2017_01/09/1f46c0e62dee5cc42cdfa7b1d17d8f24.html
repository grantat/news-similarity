Steelers' Ben Roethlisberger in walking boot, says he'll play vs. Chiefs

USA TODAY Sports Published 5:33 p.m. ET Jan. 8, 2017 | Updated 3 hours ago

Pittsburgh Steelers quarterback Ben Roethlisberger (7) throws the ball against the Miami Dolphins during the first half in the AFC Wild Card playoff football game at Heinz Field.(Photo: Geoff Burke, USA TODAY Sports)

Ben Roethlisberger didn't emerge unscathed from the Pittsburgh Steelers' 30-12 win over the Miami Dolphins, but the quarterback doesn't expect to be hampered for long.

Roethlisberger was wearing a walking boot after the game and said he suffered an ankle injury on Pittsburgh's penultimate offensive play of the game, when he threw an interception to Dolphins cornerback Xavien Howard.

The fourth-quarter play on his last pass attempt in which Ben Roethlisberger was hurt, via @EthanHesse. pic.twitter.com/Eril81qHeY

— Adam Schefter (@AdamSchefter) January 8, 2017

But Roethlisberger added he expects to play Sunday when the Steelers face the Kansas City Chiefs in the divisional round.

“You’re always worried about being hurt," Roethlisberger said in his postgame news conference. "But I’ll be out there next week.”

Roethlisberger added that he was not surprised to still be in the game despite the Steelers' lead.

"I love being out there with the guys," Roethlisberger said.
