    Golden Globes 2017 Highlights and Analysis

    Read highlights and analysis from two Times reporters and a critic on the 2017 Golden Globes. “La La Land” won a record seven awards, including best picture comedy. “Moonlight” won for best picture drama. JAN. 8, 2017

    1. Tamara Best

      8:02 PM ET

    2. Dave Itzkoff

      Reporter

      7:33 PM ET

      Good evening, everyone! The 2017 awards season begins tonight with the 74th Golden Globes, and we’re here to keep you company, to comment and to commiserate throughout the whole three-hour show. I’m Dave Itzkoff, a culture reporter for The New York Times. I’m joined by Wesley Morris, critic-at-large, and Melena Ryzik, a fellow reporter and awards-season veteran.

    3. Melena Ryzik

      Reporter

      7:33 PM ET

      Thanks Dave and hello all! Happy to be part of this viewing party. Jimmy Fallon is hosting, which means it will likely veer towards silly escapism, instead of the social wit of the Tina Fey-Amy Poehler years or the barbs of the cringy Ricky Gervais era. Expect an opening song-and-dance number in the vein of “La La Land,” Damien Chazelle’s musical love letter to Los Angeles.


      But Fallon also knows his Hollywood audience, so he’ll doubtless riff on the president-elect – perhaps to atone further for his softball “Tonight Show” interview with his fellow NBC personality during the campaign. (He already did some penance, opposite Tina on “Saturday Night Live.”) And given the unusually diverse spate of TV nominees – including newcomers like Issa Rae of “Insecure” and Riz Ahmed of “The Night Of” – expect some potentially political moments, stirring or grandstanding too.

    5. Wesley Morris

      Critic

      7:34 PM ET

      Hello Dave and Melena and everybody else. This is going to be fun – even when some of what we’re watching threatens to be the opposite of that. The Golden Globes have always been the chillest of these big awards extravaganzas. They’re first up, for one thing. The stakes are lower (or they used to be), the vibe boozier, the winners occasionally more baffling.


      The top movies, expectations-wise, are “La La Land” and “Moonlight.” But, you know, ANYTHING CAN HAPPEN (especially in the TV categories). But maybe it won’t. Which actually, might be a weird comfort since anything is happening everywhere else. Let’s do it!
