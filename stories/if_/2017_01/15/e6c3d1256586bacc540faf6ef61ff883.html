1 dead, 3 wounded in Saturday city shootings

A man is dead, and three others were wounded including a 16-year-old boy, in shootings Saturday morning on the South and West sides.

Shortly after 8 a.m., two men were standing in the 1100 block of South Francisco Avenue when a black sedan pulled up, said police.

A person exited the sedan and opened fire. He then got back into the sedan and fled north on Francisco, police said.

A 27-year-old man was shot in the head and chest, and was taken to Mount Sinai Hospital, where he was pronounced dead. The other victim, 39, suffered a wound to the back. He was taken to the same hospital and listed in good condition, police said.

In the latest shooting, a 16-year-old boy was left in serious condition after being wounded in the West Englewood neighborhood, police said.

About 11:45 a.m., the boy was shot in the 1900 block of West 70th Street. Police said the boy was walking west on 70th when someone in a vehicle started shooting and continued east.

The boy suffered wounds to the upper torso and abdomen, and was taken to Advocate Christ Medical Center in Oak Lawn, police said.

About 9:45 a.m., an 18-year-old man was shot in the same neighborhood as the earlier fatal attack. That shooting happened in the 1500 block of South Hamlin Avenue, police said.

According to preliminary reports, the victim was walking up the rear porch of a building when he heard shots and was struck. He suffered a wound to the right elbow, and was taken to Mount Sinai Hospital where he was listed in good condition, police said.

No one was in custody, and police were investigating.
