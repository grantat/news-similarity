Charges dropped against mom, daughter in viral arrest video

USA Today Network Lauren Zakalik, WFAA-TV, Dallas-Fort Worth 5:08 p.m. ET Jan. 26, 2017

Jacqueline Craig, 46, of Fort Worth, Texas, as seen in Officer William Martin's body-camera footage of her arrest Dec. 21, 2016, in Fort Worth.(Photo: WFAA-TV, Dallas-Fort Worth)

FORT WORTH, Texas — Police dropped charges Thursday against a woman and her teen daughter arrested after they had called authorities for help after a choking.

Cellphone video of the arrests of Jacqueline Craig, 46, and daughter Brea Hymond, 19, spread online in December. Craig had called Fort Worth police Dec. 21 to report that a man, later identified as Itamar Vardi, had choked her 7-year-old son while accusing him of littering.

Officer William Martin responded to the call, and an argument between Martin and Craig escalated. Martin eventually pulled out his Taser and put Craig, Hymond and a third female in custody, charging them with resisting arrest, search or transport.

“This decision didn’t happen in a vacuum," Chief Joel Fitzgerald of Fort Worth Police said about not pressing charge. "My decision to pull back these charges was something that I thought was right, something that we owed the community and the Craig family in particular.”

Martin received a 10-day suspension without pay and returned to work this week. In the more than a month since Craig's arrest occurred, multiple protests were organized calling for the officer to be fired.

Instead, Martin won't return to his assignment in the Rock Garden neighborhood where the arrests occurred and will go through what Fitzgerald called "a tremendous amount of retraining."

Vardi was issued a citation for assault by contact.

No parts of the case will be referred to a grand jury, according to a Tarrant County spokesperson. Fort Worth police have opened an investigation into the unauthorized release of body-camera footage from Martin that also surfaced Thursday on YouTube.

In the days following the arrest, Fitzgerald said he was disappointed after seeing the viral arrest video and said Martin acted rude toward the family. He stopped short of labeling it as racism.
