People are ripping a Brewers pitcher for tweeting his birth control views

By: Charles Curtis | January 13, 2017 10:56 am Follow @bycharlescurtis

On Thursday night, actress Jessica Chastain tweeted this in response to the Senate’s attempts to defund the Affordable Care Act:

#BirthControl is no longer covered by health insurance. Congrats USA, you're doing your part to keep women out of the work force. #smfh 😠

— Jessica Chastain (@jes_chastain) January 13, 2017

Milwaukee Brewers pitcher Matt Garza responded with this tweet …

It's called abstinence, a word that has been forgotten amongst this generation… it's the best contraceptive… #juatsaying https://t.co/OgKg1qQmln

— Matt garza (@Gdeuceswild) January 13, 2017

… Which angered a lot of people on Twitter. Here’s a sampling:

@Gdeuceswild so a married couple, after having a few kids, needs to just end their intimate life together? That's absurd and wrong.

— Amie (@amiehartnett) January 13, 2017

.@Gdeuceswild not that any woman should have to explain anything to you, but BC helps with things that aren't related to sex or babies!

— vaccine haver (@kerrence) January 13, 2017

"Hmm," says Matt Garza, "time to school this actress on birth control. And…send." *flexes hands*

— jon greenberg (@jon_greenberg) January 13, 2017

oh, matt garza, I'm a brewers fan, but, buddy, birth control is used for so many more things than just preventing pregnancy

— allison s ⚯͛ (@allisoncc) January 13, 2017

Matt Garza, pitcher/women's health expert

— Brian Coulter (@PhilaBCoulter) January 13, 2017

Thankfully Matt Garza is here to remind women how to live their lives. https://t.co/jX5nu5FTWT

— Adam Wells (@adamwells1985) January 13, 2017

Matt Garza has 6 children. Wants to talk about abstinence being the best birth control.

— Joanna (@HumandChuck) January 13, 2017

Garza was actually once part of a town hall on teenage pregnancy — in 2009 he appeared with Bristol Palin and actress Hayden Panettiere to discuss prevention.
