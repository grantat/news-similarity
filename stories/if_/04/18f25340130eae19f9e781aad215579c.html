Eric Trump Says David Duke 'Deserves a Bullet'

Republican candidate Donald Trump's son Eric took a verbal shot at David Duke and his white nationalists supporters Thursday, saying the former Ku Klux Klan leader "deserves a bullet."

Trump told conservative radio host Ross Kaminsky, of Denver's 630 KHOW, that Duke and his supporters are "horrible people," and praised his father for his attempts at outreach to the black community.

"Ross, it's disgusting, and by the way, if I said exactly what you said, I'd get killed for it, but I think I'll say it anyway, the guy does deserve a bullet," Trump said of Duke.

"In fact, I commend my father. My father is the first Republican who's gone out and said, 'Listen, what's happened to the African-American community is horrible and I'm going to take care of it.'"

Earlier this week, The Crusader, a prominent white nationalist newspaper linked to the KKK, endorsed Trump for president. The Republican nominee and his campaign have renounced the support of white nationalists.

Eric Trump said as president his father would put a "disproportionate amount of attention on the inner cities" to address minority issues, he and criticized President Obama for allegedly not doing more to address gun violence in Chicago.

Clinton Urges Voters To Imagine Trump Presidency

PITTSBURGH — With only four days to go until the election, Hillary Clinton urged voters here to consider what a Donald Trump presidency would be like based on his history with women and minority groups.

Speaking inside Heinz Field, the home of the Pittsburgh Steelers, Clinton argued that "we already know a lot" about how the Republican nominee would govern, if elected.

She described Trump as someone who "demeans women, insults African Americans and Latinos, demonizes immigrants and Muslims, and pits people against each other. Instead of pulling us together."

Clinton also asked the crowd to contemplate "what it would mean to entrust the nuclear codes to someone with a very thin skin who lashes out at anyone who challenges him."

Pittsburgh native Mark Cuban, who introduced Clinton, used a lengthy word exercise to rip Trump. He used each letter of the word "trust" to explain why voters shouldn't put their faith in the real estate mogul. He also criticized him for talking too much.

"You cannot be president of the United States if you don't know when to shut up," Cuban said to laughs.

Trump Releases Another Very Bullish Electoral Map

Donald Trump's campaign on Friday released another electoral map that painted an even more bullish outlook on the Republican nominee's chances than an already optimistic map released last week.

The map, which is out of touch with the NBC News battleground map and nearly every other polling expert, included eight tossup states: New Mexico, Colorado, Minnesota, Wisconsin, Michigan, New Hampshire Pennsylvania and Virginia. Three states in the Trump campaign's previously released map -- Ohio, Maine, and North Carolina -- were moved from tossups to lean GOP.

"If this trend continues until Election Day, we can bank on 266 electoral votes," the memo to supporters said. That would leave them just four electoral votes shy of clinching the White House.

Polls show the race has tightened in recent days, though the Trump map ignores that his designated tossup states favor Hillary Clinton.

Florida, North Carolina, Ohio, Utah and Arizona are among the states the Trump campaign has designated lean or likely Republican that NBC calls toss ups.

Pharrell to Black Voters: Prove You're Not Just a 'Minority'

Pop star Pharrell Williams hit the campaign trail with Hillary Clinton in North Carolina Thursday, challenging African American voters to prove that they are more than just "minorities."

"This country hasn't always been fair to all people — especially my culture," Williams said, later adding, "If you've ever been called a 'minority,' go out and vote and show everybody that you're actually really the majority."

Williams said Clinton exemplifies a new form of leadership that's based on listening to voters instead of speaking at them, and he praised the Democratic nominee for exemplifying how to shatter glass ceilings.

"How dare anyone question a woman's ability? Every person on this planet was brought into this world by a woman," Williams said. "For once, when it comes to the nuke buttons, I'd like to see it commanded by a women who knows what it's like to physically bring life into this world, and who will think of life holistically before she decides to take them out."

"I want to live in a world where a woman's ability are respected and not ever questioned," he said.

Dems Poke Fun at Melania Trump's Convention Speech Scandal

Melania Trump is making a rare solo appearance on the campaign trail Thursday near Philadelphia, and Democrats are using the opportunity to take a playful dig at Donald Trump's wife.

In a tongue-in-cheek news release, the Pennsylvania Democratic Party suggested they "obtained an advance copy of the speech Melania Trump is scheduled to deliver today in Chester County."

The address, however, redirects to a White House transcript of the speech that first lady Michelle Obama gave last month in Manchester, New Hampshire.

The swipe at Melania Trump is a direct nod to when she came under sharp criticism for giving a speech at the Republican National Convention in July that closely resembled one given by Michelle Obama at the Democratic National Convention in 2008.

A Trump organization staffer took responsibility for Melania Trump's bungled address, allowing the issue to die down, although Donald Trump poked fun at the controversy during a white-tie dinner in New York last month.
