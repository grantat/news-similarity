    2016 gift and gadget guide

    By Hayley Tsukayama
    Nov. 11, 2016
    Share on Facebook
    Share on Twitter
    Share on Google Plus
    Share via Email

    Looking to get the gee-whiz factor in your holiday gift selections? This is the guide for you, with a look at the year’s top smartphones. A whirl through the video game world, for children and big kids, too. You’ll find gadgets and gear and goods for the home – tested in depth to point you to the best values and of course a few worth-it splurges.

    Smartphones

    iPhone 7/iPhone 7 Plus

    Pro: An elegant and surprisingly substantial upgrade to the iPhone.

    Con: The lack of a headphone jack could be a deal-breaker for many people.

    Price: The iPhone 7 starts at $649.99, unlocked. Check carriers for pricing options.

    Description: On paper, the latest iPhone doesn’t look like much of an upgrade. But many small improvements to Apple’s flagship phone, including some waterproofing and an upgraded camera for more stable pictures and better low-light snaps, make it well worth the update. That’s particularly true if you have an older iPhone, such as the 5s or earlier. The 7s is snappier, looks better and takes better pictures than its predecessors. The new iPhones do lack one big feature: a standard headphone jack. The phone comes with a pair of headphones that plug into its Lightning port, also known as the charging port. So, yes, that means that without buying extra wireless headphones you can’t power up your phone and listen privately to music at the same time. For long commuters and desk DJs, that may be a deal-breaker; for those who don’t listen to music or podcasts for long stretches, it’s less of a problem. If you’re interested in the larger iPhone 7 Plus, you should know that the stand-out feature is its dual camera sensor, which Apple has used to make the zoom much better than any other phone camera on the market. The iPhone 7 Plus also has a longer battery life.

    Smartphones

    Samsung Galaxy S7/S7 edge

    Pro: Beautiful styling and top-notch performance.

    Con: Can be a little fragile.

    Price: Starts at $669.99, unlocked. Check carriers for pricing options.

    Description: It’s been a rough year for Samsung fans, who may have had their hearts set on the now-defunct Galaxy Note 7. But the Galaxy S7 and S7 edge are both excellent phones worth a look on their own. These models haven’t suffered from the same battery woes as the Note, and both have powerful processors, gorgeous screens and beautiful styling. The phones both have an always-on display, which keeps critical information such as the time on your screen without draining the battery. The S7 edge, in particular, is great to read or watch video on thanks to a screen that drapes over the sides of the phone like the edge of an infinity pool. The display is 5.5 inches — the same as the iPhone 7 Plus — but it feels somehow more expansive thanks to those edges. Without a case, both the S7 and the S7 edge can be a little slippery and prone to accidents; they also run a little (though not alarmingly) warm under a heavy load. But both models are among the best Android phones out there, with a few features such as wireless charging that you don’t get from the iPhone.

    Smartphones

    Google Pixel/Pixel XL

    Pro: Pure, unadulterated Google.

    Con: A non-waterproof Verizon “exclusive.”

    Price: Starts at $749.99, unlocked. Check Verizon for pricing options.

    Description: Google seems to be getting more serious about its smartphones with the introduction of the Pixel and Pixel XL. While this phone was built by HTC, it was completely designed by Google to show off the full extent of the Android system it created and its fancy new software, including the voice-controlled Assistant. The Pixels will get the latest updates of Google’s software first, and both models come out of the box with the company’s latest system, Android Nougat. The Google Assistant shows a lot of promise, providing much of the same information and features as Siri in a smoother and more conversational style. It isn’t perfect, but it is impressive — especially for a first try at creating an artificial intelligence assistant. As a piece of hardware, the Pixel is slim and sits well in hand — its fingerprint reader is on the back, which makes the sensor easy to use while holding the phone. The only downsides are the camera, which while good isn’t quite as crisp or stable as the cameras on competing phones, and the fact that this phone is not waterproof. It’s also a Verizon “exclusive,” though buyers can pick up an unlocked version from Google itself to operate on other networks.

    Smartphones

    LG V20

    Pro: A removable battery.

    Con: Battery life isn’t what you may expect from a phone this big and pricey.

    Price: Starts at $649.99, unlocked. Check carriers for pricing options.

    Description: LG has struggled a bit to produce a solid smartphone, but the V20 is a fairly good offering from the company and has a key feature that many other phones lack: a removable battery. Battery life on this phone is not as long-lasting as on some of its similarly sized competitors, but being able to swap out the battery for a fresh one is a perk. This phone is also supposed to be a bit more rugged than its competitors, and designed to withstand a good drop or two — just don’t get carried away. It’s not the world’s prettiest phone, and has a utilitarian look about it. Yet while the V20’s styling may not be worth writing home about, it is a bold design with very square edges and a dual camera on the back with an added wide-angle lens that gives you the opportunity to take some pretty expansive and impressive shots. LG also boasts that this phone has excellent audio, and includes a pair of B&O Play headphones in the box, to show off its audio quality even more. (Because, yes, this phone does have a headphone jack.)

    Smartphones

    Huawei Honor 8

    Pro: Goes beyond a basic smartphone for a very nice price.

    Con: Not a name brand you probably know.

    Price: Starts at $390, unlocked. Check carriers for pricing options.

    Description: Huawei is probably not a household name for most smartphone buyers, but this Chinese firm is certainly starting to impress with the quality of its phones at a much more palatable price. The Huawei Honor 8 may not have the polish of an iPhone or a Samsung Galaxy, but it packs a decent punch and manages to look refined At $390, the Huawei Honor 8 delivers a 5.7-inch screen and reasonably good performance — certainly better than you might expect from a mid-range device. It also has a fairly good camera, with a dual camera that should please even picky casual photographers. It’s certainly good enough for an Instagram feed. This phone’s main drawback is its short battery life. But, hey, you had to expect some sort of tradeoff for the dip in price. Overall, you’re getting what you paid for — and then some.

    Smartphones

    Moto Z Play

    Pro: Its modular build brings something different to the table.

    Con: Lacks some of the polish of its competitors.

    Price: Starts at $450, unlocked. Check carriers for pricing options.

    Description: The Motorola Z PLay offers a modular take on the smartphone, giving users the option to buy accessories to snap on and off the phone to further customize it. If you want a better camera or a better speaker, you can buy the appropriate “Moto Mod” and amp up your phone’s specs that way. It’s a little gimmicky, sure, but a nice option if you have a very specific need for your smartphone and don’t mind making its already slightly bulky frame a little bulkier for some personalized perks. Overall, the phone performs well — it handles normal tasks without a noticeable stutter and will serve your smartphone needs. This is a more mid-range phone, so it probably isn’t as suited to heavy-duty mobile gaming or other demanding tasks but perfectly good for streaming music and video. The Z Play also, unlike other Moto Z phones, retains its headphone jack. It also has an impressive battery life for its size and can probably go more than a day of normal use without having to be plugged in.

    Smartphones

    OnePlus 3

    Pro: Budget-friendly with few compromises

    Con: Only compatible with AT&T and T-Mobile

    Price: Starts at $400, unlocked. Check carriers for pricing options.

    Description: Another phone from a most cost-effective Chinese firm, the OnePlus 3 is a good option for people who want a decent smartphone but don’t want to break the bank to get it. In terms of performance, this phone can compete with rivals that cost far more than its $400 pricetag, and should make people rethink what they can expect from a “cheap” smartphone. The processing power of this phone is particularly impressive for its price, and it has a nice-looking aluminum body that feels premium. Its camera is also excellent, taking very good pictures in bright and low light. The OnePlus 3 also has a Dash Charging function that makes it easy to top up the phone’s long-lasting battery when it runs low on power. One big caveat: It is compatible only with GSM carriers, such as AT&T and T-Mobile.

    Adult Games

    Civilization VI

    Pro: Rewards players who spend the time with it

    Con: Not for fans of a shoot ’em up.

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "Civilization," in which players choose a world leader and set out to conquer the world one parcel of land at a time, is one of those franchises that manages to stay familiar while never getting too stale. With the latest installment, the developers have done it again, by introducing what is actually a fairly radical change without giving devoted fans a case of whiplash. The big change is that the cities, always the lifeblood of a player’s civilization, are now able to sprawl and allow players to actually do some urban planning. Districts, a new feature in this game, can specialize in things such as trade, education and military training and get boosts from the terrain around them. Placement, in other words, really matters. This change requires players to think more critically about their resources and consider how they want to fit their cities together — almost like a jigsaw puzzle.

    Adult Games

    Overwatch

    Pro: Pure team-based fun.

    Con: Arguably better with your friends.

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "Overwatch" has only been out for several months, but it’s already creating its own culture — and for good reason. The online multiplayer game lets players pick from a variety of unique characters, which then work together as a team toward their goals. Lifespans are short in this game, and players can switch between characters often (read: between deaths) to provide different sorts of support for their team throughout the matches. The game is arguably only as good, however, as the people you play it with. If you don’t have a thick skin or are unfamiliar with the culture of online gaming, you may want to make sure to play with a buddy or two, at least at the beginning. The community has been fairly well-behaved, but coordination and communication are important, and it’s generally far more fun to play with the devils you know.

    Adult Games

    Deus Ex: Mankind Divided

    Pro: Action-packed, good-looking and fun.

    Con: The story may leave some people wanting more.

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "Deus Ex: Mankind Divided" is set in a time after a global incident caused all humans with cybernetic implants to lash out with unspeakable violence. As the game’s own enhanced protagonist, Adam Jensen, players must fight their way through a world that calls for combat of all kinds, from high-tech hacking to good old-fashioned brawling. The weak point of this game is likely its story, which takes some interesting, even convoluted twists and has drawn complaints about an abrupt ending. It’s true that the game could grapple more deeply with questions about humanity and machinery that are inherent in the plot. But the story at least doesn’t distract too much from some seriously solid gameplay that is fun and rewarding.

    Adult Games

    Battlefield 1

    Pro: A rich alternative history to a fascinating period

    Con: Multiplayer mode may take some time to develop

    Price: $59.99

    Description: War is cruel, and "Battlefield 1" takes players back to one of the most brutal and unforgiving wars ever — World War I. In an age where the industry seems obsessed with space marines and future tech, it’s a bold step. And it’s one that pays off for "Battlefield," which takes pains to recreate (as much as is possible) the muddy, bloody experience of trench warfare. The game actually flits between several perspectives, giving players a small taste of the settings and people caught up in this pivotal conflict. The game is still fairly young, and so it’s hard to say how the atmosphere of the multiplayer will evolve. Multiplayer is a huge part of any "Battlefield" game, and so this a major component of the game that’s still, essentially, under development. That said, the modes are fun, and familiar to anyone who’s played any iteration of this franchise before.

    Adult Games

    Mafia III

    Pro: Gives players flexibility and plenty of room to run

    Con: Starts off stronger than it ends

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "Mafia III" puts players in the shoes of Lincoln Clay, a biracial gangster recently returned from Vietnam and looking for revenge against the Sicilian mob that took out his crime family in a stylized version of New Orleans. New Bordeaux, as the game’s city is called, is divided into districts and players have to conquer each part of the city as they build their own empire from the ashes of the old one. "Mafia III" gives Clay his own lieutenants, which also sets up an internal power struggle to offset the larger battle. "Mafia III" starts off strong but can get bogged down as players work their way through the story and the city and can eventually lose some of its momentum. The game has missed some opportunities, particularly to shine a light on the era that the developers very consciously chose. But on balance, "Mafia III" is still worth a look for players who like the franchise and those with an appreciation for a good setting.

    Adult Games

    XCOM 2

    Pro: The game keeps up a tense, exciting mood from start to finish

    Con: Some cosmetic hiccups can cause problems during the game

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "XCOM 2," a turn-based strategy game, carries over all the tension and high-stakes play of its predecessor, "XCOM." The world, conquered by aliens, is in need of your armed resistance and careful planning for the sake of humanity. But be warned: this game is not designed to be easy. It is hard and purposefully random, sometimes to the point of cruelty — but not necessarily in a bad way. Just expect to feel your fair share of frustration, and don’t get too attached to any of the characters you direct. "XCOM 2" is a solid game, though at times there can be some cosmetic hiccups that keep players from getting a full view of the map around them — critical information to have when creating a battle strategy. But these problems are likely to be fixed in future patches and shouldn’t prevent anyone from picking the game up. The random nature of the game also gives it great replay value, since you’re unlikely to get exactly the same experience twice.

    Adult Games

    Unravel

    Pro: This innovative game provides some interesting challenges

    Con: It may be a little too quirky for some.

    Price: $19.99

    Description: "Unravel" is perhaps best-described as a game of pure whimsy. This title puts players in the place of a small creature made of Yarn — Yarny, actually — who must make his way through the world using only the yarn contained in his own body. Players can use the free end of Yarny to swing from branch to branch and attach themselves to points in the environment to get around a host of obstacles. As long as you can find another supply of yarn, you’re fine to keep unraveling yourself to move forward. This tender game actually does have a plot that touches on longing, love, loss and nostalgia, but it’s mostly a meditative puzzle game that requires just a dab of critical thinking. Puzzles can get a bit repetitive, and the controls are not always as responsive as players might like. But overall, it is a sweet tale and one worth checking out if you like a slower pace to your play.

    Adult Games

    Battlezone VR

    Pro: You get to drive a hovertank!

    Con: Requires the PS VR, which means an extra expense.

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "Battlezone VR" puts players in the cockpit of a hovertank, thanks to the PlayStation VR virtual reality headset. The game, which one can play seated, places players on a stylized version of a battle field but still manages to convince your senses that you’re actually driving and moving around in your very own enormous vehicle. A reboot of an old Atari franchise, the game certainly retains an arcade feel that should appeal to plenty of players. Players can shoot enemies and take territory in battles, all while working toward a broader victory. It can feel at times like you’re constantly under siege, particularly with the VR, so it’s probably a game best left to short bursts of play. Players can participate in online matches, or stay offline if they prefer.

    Adult Games

    Inside

    Pro: Inside is a unique and dark game that is utterly engaging.

    Con: As an indie game, it lacks that blockbuster smash.

    Price: $19.99

    Description: "Inside" is going to make a lot of top independent game lists this year, and with good cause. This puzzle-platformer has a unique feel to it, and deftly sketches out a terrifying world. Players inhabit a small boy who’s running from guards in a very eerie world inhabited by zombie-like people. Using the boy’s powers of mind control, players can solve puzzles as he makes his way through the environment. The game has a dark humor augmented by its indie aesthetic, which while certainly not in the style of a photorealistic blockbuster, does help set the mood for what is, ultimately, a pretty horrifying world. Plot twists add to the excitement of the game, which admittedly isn’t very long — a dedicated player could probably knock it out in an evening. That said, even though it’s short, it’s well worth the time and money to play it.

    Kid Games

    Yo-Kai Watch 2 (Bony Spirits and Fleshy Souls)

    Pro: Quirky characterizations and charming writing.

    Con: Can get a bit repetitive, particularly for those who’ve played the first game.

    Price: $39.99

    Description: A Pokemon-style game, "Yo-kai Watch 2" asks kids, or players of all ages, to search the world for spirits, called Yo-kai, to fight and gain the right to summon on command. This second installment of the series comes in two versions — "Bony Spirits" and "Fleshy Souls" — which represent two factions of a civil war within the universe. (Don’t worry, it’s quite civil as civil wars go.) These versions of the game also add online multiplayer battles, which is a welcome addition but worth noting for parents who want to keep an eye on their kids’ online interactions. Combat in general is fun and engaging, though it can feel like a bit of a slog if you don’t take a break every now and then. Overall, though, this is a worthy sequel to a fun franchise and worth checking out.

    Kid Games

    Dragon Quest Builders

    Pro: A classic franchise meets a “Minecraft”-style building world with good results.

    Con: Not necessarily action-packed.

    Price: $59.99

    Description: "Minecraft" has become the gold standard of “builder” games, which let players create their own worlds from materials they mine and hone into the tools of their choosing. "Dragon Quest Builders" could have easily been a "Minecraft" clone that just happened to be set in the world of the classic series, but the developers have deftly avoided that trap and made it into a full-fledged concept on its own. Building is a big part of this game, and players are asked to construct buildings in four different main areas throughout the story, to serve different purposes. Each of the areas has its own rules about what should be built and how, and each advances the game in its own way. But story — an area that "Minecraft" has always largely ignored — also plays a big part in "Dragon Quest Builders." The role-playing elements of the central series shine through in this spinoff as well, making it distinctly different from most builder games.

    Kid Games

    Disney Art Academy for Nintendo 3DS

    Pro: Good, clean fun that’s sneakily educational

    Con: The stylus can feel clunky at times.

    Price: $29.99

    Description: Kids can tap their inner Walt Disney in this game, which offers basic instruction on how to sketch characters from the Disneyverse while secretly teaching kids (gasp!) some of the basics of drawing. Younger players may particularly like learning how to draw characters they recognize, even if the game does stick mostly to the fundamentals. It is true that this game will not make you a stunning artist overnight — or, indeed, replace a good drawing class in any meaningful way. The stylus on the 3DS doesn’t always feel like the most precise tool. And the game itself can lose its luster over time. But it is fun, and could help get players thinking about how to apply their creative skills to their own characters down the line. For parents, it’s a purchase they can make without worrying about what content may show up in the game.

    Kid Games

    Lego Star Wars: The Force Awakens

    Pro: A witty send-up and love letter to the Star Wars franchise.

    Con: The humor is probably a little mature for very young players

    Price: $50

    Description: The Lego video game adaptations of popular movies are always fun for the whole family, and "Lego Star Wars: The Force Awakens" is no exception. As always, Lego has adapted this game to be part love letter and part parody, staying true to the original material while also putting a snarky spin on it. The game itself is a combination of action, adventure and puzzler — a well-rounded game that should have appeal for most types of players. The game carries a rating of 10 and up, which is appropriate for its humor. While things never get blue or bawdy, there is some humor that will appeal more to older children (and adults) than your little ones. There is also some cartoon violence, though certainly nothing that could be called disturbing — just objects dissolving into piles of Legos.

    Kid Games

    Skylanders Imaginators

    Pro: Spurs greater creativity with the best

    Con: The game has stuck to a successful formula, but a formula nonetheless

    Price: $74.99

    Description: "Skylanders" is a perennial favorite for young players, who can play with the characters both in-game and in the real world -- the game comes with real figurines that you can collect and play with offline as well. With "Skylanders Imaginators," the game has taken another creative leap forward, giving players the option to design their own Skylanders in the game, choosing from several attributes in a number of combinations. Core gameplay in "Imaginators" is very similar to past games, which means it will feel familiar (maybe even perhaps a little boring) to those who have played the series in the past. But there are some updates, including very powerful figures that can really tip a battle in your favor when used judiciously. Parents should know that there are some micro-transactions in this game, as well as the option to have custom characters 3D printed and sent to your doorstep. You can play together to avoid surprise charges — but also play together because it’s fun.

    Kid Games

    Song of the Deep

    Pro: Beautiful, interesting exploratory game.

    Con: Gets a little less challenging -- but no less fun -- toward the end.

    Price: $14.99

    Description: "Song of the Deep" puts players in the place of a woman named Merryn, who’s on an underwater quest to save her father. The sense of movement in this underwater game is excellent and gives players a distinct experience and environment for players of all ages. Cerebral and oddly soothing, this game offers a lot of bang for its buck with a lot of playtime and surprises to uncover. It’s true that the puzzles in "Song of the Deep" can get a little samey-samey after a while and that some of the challenges are not as difficult in the later game. But the overall game provides a strong challenge and a great ambience for younger players — and it’s not bad for older players either.

    Kid Games

    Forza Horizon 3

    Pro: A beautiful game that should appeal to speed demons of all ages.

    Con: Not a gearhead? This may not be for you.

    Price: $59.99

    Description: Of all the games released this year, "Forza Horizon 3" may be one of the easiest on the eyes, with its large fleet of cars rendered in loving detail for virtual races across the varied terrains of Australia. Car enthusiasts (or even those who appreciate a pretty game) should be happy with the variety of wheels available, as well as the many, many options to customize your ride so that it fits exactly what you want. In this iteration of the game, players are encouraged to travel from festival to festival in their gorgeous cars, and to do some hard driving along the way to earn money, unlock achievements and rock out to the game’s impressive soundtrack. It’s fantasy indulgence at its finest — you can crash with no consequences — and straightforward, fast and furious fun for players of all ages.

    Kid Games

    Paper Mario: Color Splash

    Pro: Visually stunning and immersive.

    Con: Suffers from the same weaknesses as previous installments.

    Price: 59.99

    Description: From a visual standpoint, it’s hard not to be impressed with "Paper Mario: Color Splash," which, as one may intuit from the title, delivers a big and richly hued world while retaining its own unique paper cutout style. The art style and music add so much to this game’s charm that it’s easy to get swept up in its aesthetics. The gameplay, however, can get a little boring — players have to collect in-game Battle Cards that let them attack enemies, and engaging in fight after fight can get tedious at times. This is a problem that has shown up in previous "Paper Mario" games as well; if action is what you’re looking for, this may not be the game for you. That said, Wii U owners looking for a nice game to pick up this holiday may find it worth a play, particularly if they’re fans of the series already.

    Kid Games

    Thumper

    Pro: A rhythmic and beautiful game that works in VR or out of it.

    Con: Its simplicity may be a little too simple for some players.

    Price: $19.99

    Description: Mesmerizing may be the best word for "Thumper," which is one of the games supported by the PlayStation VR headset this holiday season. (Support for other headsets, the HTC Vive and the Oculus Rift, are on the roadmap as well.) It’s a little hard to explain "Thumper," which is all about finding your rhythm, as you take on the perspective of a metal beetle racing down a long track toward menacing figures in the distance. As odd as that may sound, "Thumper" is addictive, particularly in VR, when you can be completely immersed by the music and entirely bent on the next twist or obstacle in your way. "Thumper" advertises itself as a “rhythm violence” game, but there isn’t exactly a lot of overt violence — though the trippy backgrounds may get a little scary for very young players who spook easily. On the flip side, older players who are looking for a complex challenge may find its gameplay too simple. "Thumper" is best for players in the middle, who want something engaging.

    Gifts under $100

    BlueAnt Pump 2 HD Multi-Sport Bluetooth Headphones

    Pro: Durable, waterproof construction.

    Con: Sound quality isn’t the absolute best.

    Price: $99.95

    Description: BlueAnt’s Pump 2 HD headphones are made for working out. They are wireless with a decent range that won’t fritz out on you in the rain. (In theory, you could take them swimming — though you’d have to be pretty near your phone at all times.) These headphones should work with any device that also has Bluetooth, making them versatile in more ways than one. The sound quality is clear, but probably better suited for accompanying a run than a deep appreciation of the music. Audiophiles should look elsewhere. But if you’re looking for something waterproof, wireless, relatively cheap and sturdy, this is a good pair to consider.

    Gifts under $100

    Moshi iGlaze Ion

    Pro: Stylish, practical and convenient.

    Con: Just for the iPhone 6 and iPhone 6S.

    Price: $99.95

    Description: Few things panic an always-connected person more than a yellow or — horrors! — red battery indicator on their smartphones. But one easy way to keep yourself from feeling that pressure is to get a phone case that comes with an extra battery built in. Moshi’s iGlaze Ion is an excellent, stylish and fairly lightweight case that can offer you extra juice and some peace of mind. This battery pack should get you through the day and then some and is particularly comforting to have on the road, when you’re not sure when you’ll next be able to get to an outlet. The iGlaze Ion is currently only available for the iPhone 6 and iPhone 6S, however, so owners of other phones will have to look elsewhere.

    Gifts under $100

    Pencil by Fifty Three

    Pro: A good-looking, simple stylus.

    Con: A little pricey.

    Price: $49.95-$59.95

    Description: It’s becoming more of a digital world every year. But don’t you sometimes just miss the feeling of a good pencil in your hand? Pencil by Fifty Three is a tried-and-true stylus for sketchers and writers who want to split the difference between the digital and analog worlds — and who like a little heft to their implement. The Pencil comes in a variety of shades and materials, giving you some personalization options without breaking the bank. Think of it as the modern equivalent of a pen and pencil set. The quality can help you feel as though you’re getting your money’s worth, though honestly these can still feel a little pricey for what you get. That said, they’re nice to give as gifts — a splurge for someone else that they may not treat themselves to. Just be sure to get a case with a loop, since Pencil by Fifty Three does not come with a clip.

    Gifts under $100

    Fitbit Flex 2

    Pro: Lightweight and unobtrusive.

    Con: Lacks some tracking features more serious athletes may want.

    Price: $99.95

    Description: The Fitbit Flex 2 improves upon its predecessor by being slimmer and waterproof, meaning that it can stay with you all day, even while doing the dishes or taking a lap or 25 in the pool. Like other health trackers, it will also give you reminders to move, track your workouts and — of course — keep track of your daily steps. If you’re not looking for a full-fledged smartwatch, but are mostly interested in fitness tracking, this is a good option. The Fitbit Flex will offer you call and text notifications, so you can keep a handle on the communication coming through your phone, even if you can’t compose or place calls.

    Gifts under $100

    JBL Clip 2

    Pro: Waterproof and compact.

    Con: Not for huge spaces.

    Price: $59.95

    Description: Bluetooth speakers are often a welcome gift, since they are great for parties all year round. The JBL Clip 2 is a wonderful, compact little speaker that will serve small spaces well, whether you’re trying to add some mood music to a corner of your party or just looking for a speaker that can help cheer you up in the kitchen. The JBL Clip 2 is also waterproof, meaning that it functions well as a shower radio, in case you need some tunes to get you going in the morning. Of course, that also means that’s it’s a winner at the pool, since it can stand up to splashes and the occasional immersion. It’s not the loudest speaker on the market, but it is an excellent value for its price.

    Gifts under $100

    Star Wars Force Band by Sphero for BB-8

    Description: Virtual reality is a big theme this holiday season, and Sony’s PlayStation VR headset is probably going to be the best buy for most people.     Pro: High-quality, truly immersive virtual reality.

    Con: Requires a pretty impressive computer to work.

    Price: $799.00

    Description: HTC’s Vive headset offers the best virtual reality experience on the market right now, thanks to its well-designed headset and controllers, which let users immerse themselves in digital, interactive worlds. Both also require a pretty powerful gaming computer to work, which adds a hidden cost to the price of VR. But the HTC Vive offers the best overall package and the most immersive fun — as long as you’ve got the room, the money and the right computer.
