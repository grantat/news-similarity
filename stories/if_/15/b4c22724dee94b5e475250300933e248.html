The animals and acrobats are moving in, and once again the Blackhawks and Bulls are headed out.

The United Center tenants have reached that point in their schedules when they are forced to vacate their home at 1901 W. Madison for two weeks or so while the circus takes over.

The disruption in the schedule — which dates to when the Hawks and Bulls played at the Chicago Stadium — has often proved problematic as the teams endure grueling trips that have had negative impacts on their seasons.

But the shows won't go on.

The Tribune has learned that the Ringling Bros. and Barnum & Bailey Circus won't return to the UC after it ends its run this month. And Disney on Ice, which forces the teams to take another extended trip in February, will have its UC shows condensed to a one-week run beginning in 2018.

The news confirms a July 2015 Tribune report that Blackhawks Chairman Rocky Wirtz, who co-owns the UC with Bulls Chairman Jerry Reinsdorf, likely would let the circus and ice show contracts lapse by 2017.

"The reason we're doing this is twofold," said Terry Savarise, senior vice president of operations for the UC. "One is that in future years the Bulls and Blackhawks no longer have to endure two two-week road trips, and a subset of that is that those trips had resulted in a compression of our home schedules. There were times when you'd have four home games in a one-week period, which fans didn't like.

"So the elimination of the two-week circus run and the reduction of the Disney ice show run from two weeks to one allows us to resolve those two issues on behalf of the teams. I think the teams are pleased to have the additional flexibility in the scheduling process."

So that means one final circus trip for the Hawks, and the season-high seven-game trip begins Tuesday night in Winnipeg. After stops in Calgary, Vancouver, Edmonton, San Jose, Anaheim and Los Angeles, the Hawks return home Nov. 29 against the Panthers.

The Hawks will roll into Winnipeg on a high, having won eight of nine and carrying an 11-game points streak. That run has vaulted them to the top of the Western Conference standings at 11-3-2.

"We've put ourselves in a good spot going into it," coach Joel Quenneville said. "We always say the process is trying to get better or play the right way, (and) the trend is that. That's what we want to ... keep building upon, knowing that we have some tough opponents in their buildings and they're going to be ready."

The Hawks have gone 14-3-2 over the last three circus trips, including 3-1-2 last season.

"It's a trip in the past that has been pretty successful for us, and we've had some good moments and kind of come together as a team," winger Patrick Kane said. "It will be a big road trip for us. We'll focus on the next game in Winnipeg and take it from there."
